Trust and Relationships

In relationships, trust is like a knife. When you love and trust someone, it's like you're sharpening that knife. Imagine, when a friend says something, it might not bother you much. But if someone close says the same thing, it feels like your heart breaks. That's because the closer you are, the sharper the knife.

So, when people trust you, they're basically giving you that sharp knife that can either make or break their heart. It's like saying, "Hey, help me carve out my life with this." This goes for your family, friends, and best buddies. They know it can hurt, but they still trust you with it. If you want to avoid getting hurt, you shouldn't hand out a sharp knife to everyone you meet.

Think of people like colleagues, classmates, or flatmates as folks you share a table with but not your deepest feelings. Not everyone deserves that super sharp knife.
